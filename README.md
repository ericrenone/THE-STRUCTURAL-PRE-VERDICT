## I. The Structural Pre-Authorization Architecture

Every knowledge production system assigns standing to contributors before any specific contribution is evaluated. This assignment is not a heuristic or a convenience. It is the system's primary mechanism for managing the costs of evaluation at scale.

The Structural Pre-Authorization Architecture (SPA) names the full system by which this assignment is made. SPA operates through three mechanisms that together constitute the Three-Dimensional Gatekeeping Function. The three mechanisms are not sequential stages. They are mutually reinforcing dimensions of a single structure.

The first dimension is **Producer Authorization**: the system's determination of who is entitled to generate knowledge claims that the evaluation apparatus is obligated to process. Producer Authorization operates through credential systems, institutional affiliations, publication records in recognized venues, and membership in professional networks. It is not a binary. It is a hierarchy of authorization levels, each of which determines the system's default receptive posture toward the contributor's output before any content is examined. A contributor with maximum Producer Authorization arrives at the evaluation stage pre-endorsed: the system's default posture is to receive and process. A contributor with zero Producer Authorization — the canonical outlier — arrives pre-excluded: the system's default posture is the extraction of grounds for non-engagement.

The second dimension is **Frame Authorization**: the system's determination of which questions, methods, categories, and conclusions are intelligible as knowledge claims at all. Frame Authorization is not visible as a rule. It is visible as the accumulated structure of what is considered a well-formed research question, a credible methodology, an appropriate unit of analysis, an acceptable conclusion form. The contributor whose work falls within the authorized frame space produces outputs that the evaluation apparatus recognizes as the things it evaluates. The contributor whose work falls outside the authorized frame space produces outputs that the evaluation apparatus encounters as confused, category-misplaced, or insufficiently rigorous — not because the outputs are those things, but because the frame through which they are evaluated was not designed to process them.

The third dimension is **Solicitation Authorization**: the system's determination of whose contributions are actively invited, amplified, and circulated as theoretical contributions rather than classified as experiential reports, practitioner perspectives, or anecdote. Solicitation Authorization is the dimension through which the authorization architecture operates in the forward direction — not merely refusing reception but actively organizing the production of knowledge along authorized channels. The contributor with Solicitation Authorization has their work sought, reviewed, published, cited, and transmitted. The contributor without it produces work that enters the system at its own cost, without the amplification infrastructure that transforms a valid argument into a socially circulating knowledge claim.

The three dimensions interact: Producer Authorization determines the default receptive posture; Frame Authorization determines whether the content registers as evaluable; Solicitation Authorization determines whether the output reaches the audience that would receive it. An outlier without Producer Authorization produces arguments whose content the Frame Authorization mechanism classifies as confused, generating a ground for the Comprehension Veto. An outlier whose frame falls outside authorized space produces outputs that the Producer Authorization mechanism classifies as from an uncredentialed source, generating a ground for the Credential Wall. The three dimensions do not operate independently. Each provides grounds that the others consume.

Suleymani's (2026) three-dimensional framework was developed in the context of racialized academic gatekeeping in Global South feminist scholarship. Its generalization here follows the structural logic of the three dimensions rather than their specific instantiation in that context. The formal architecture is identical: the dimensions operate through mutual reinforcement; their combined output is not unfavorable evaluation but pre-emption of the evaluation stage; and the outlier who clears one dimension finds the other two have redefined their threshold in response.

The SPA's output for any contributor is a Pre-Authorization Level (PAL): a structural standing assigned by the three-dimensional mechanism before any specific contribution is encountered. PAL determines the IPPF structure that the suppressor arrives at any encounter pre-loaded with. The suppressor who encounters a contributor with high PAL arrives with an IPPF configured for reception: the default posture is epistemic openness, and the payoff structure assigns positive value to acknowledgment of high-quality evidence. The suppressor who encounters a contributor with low PAL — the Pre-Authorization Deficit (PAD) — arrives with an IPPF configured for non-reception: the default posture is skepticism, and the payoff structure assigns negative value to acknowledgment regardless of evidence quality.

The IPPF is not a cognitive architecture the suppressor builds from scratch in each encounter. It is the SPA's local instantiation in the suppressor's trained professional cognition.

## II. Systemic Epistemic Alterity

The outlier without Structural Pre-Authorization is not in an unfavorable position relative to credentialed contributors. They are in a different ontological category within the knowledge system's architecture. Saleh's (2026) concept of systemic epistemic alterity names this: the outlier's outsider status is systemic rather than interactionally produced. It is not the output of encounters that went badly. It is a structural attribute assigned by the Authorization Architecture before any encounter occurs.

Systemic Epistemic Alterity (SEA) has three properties that distinguish it from the PRG (Performance-Reception Gap) and the social characterization that the prior corpus analyzed.

The first property is **pre-encounter assignment**. The PRG — the gap between DNR's social appearance and its epistemic reality — is produced in encounters: the suppressor executes NR, then D presents NR's output as ETR, and the gap between what occurred and what the social environment received is the mechanism's output. SEA is not produced in encounters. It is the structural condition that causes the encounters to produce the PRG. The suppressor who executes DNR against an outlier with SEA is not creating the gap — they are the local instrument through which a pre-existing structural determination expresses itself in an individual event.

The second property is **system-wide coherence**. The PRG propagates through the Social Coalition Machine: coalition members who receive the dismissal's ETR-appearing outputs transmit them, deepening the pre-configuration in the social network. This propagation was presented by the prior corpus as the mechanism that builds toward the Terminal Nash Configuration. SEA does not require this propagation. The terminal condition is already installed in the Authorization Architecture's assignment before any individual coalition member acts. Each coalition member who participates in DNR execution is not contributing to the building of a terminal state. They are performing the already-terminal state's local expression.

The third property is **revision-resistance at the structural level**. The DNR Stability Architecture's three mechanisms produce revision-resistance at the individual-suppressor level: IPPF Reinforcement raises the revision cost for the individual suppressor; NR Consolidation habituates their processing apparatus; Coalition Entrenchment raises the social cost of revision across the network. SEA produces revision-resistance at the structural level: the Authorization Architecture's three-dimensional gatekeeping function maintains its determinations through the same mechanisms that maintain any institutional structure — credential reproduction, frame socialization, solicitation channel design. Structural revision requires changing the institution, not persuading the individual. No argument the outlier can make at the individual-suppressor level addresses the structural level at which SEA operates.

This is the deepest implication of the Pre-Configuration Theorem: the outlier who successfully persuades one suppressor — whose argument achieves genuine reception in one encounter — has not moved toward changing their PAD. The structural determination that produced the PAD is not a social characterization distributed across individual suppressors. It is a property of the Authorization Architecture itself. Individual encounters, however they resolve, leave the structural determination intact.

## III. The Structural Bubble

The Structural Bubble (SB) is the mechanism through which the Authorization Architecture self-seals against contributions that fall outside it. Rubenstein's (2026) analysis identifies four interacting layers through which the SB operates. Each layer is an independent execution site of the authorization determination. Together they constitute a self-maintaining system that does not require conscious coordination to perpetuate its exclusions.

**Layer One: Institutional Funding and Conflict of Interest.** Knowledge production requires resources. Resources flow through funding institutions whose allocation decisions are organized by the Authorization Architecture's Producer and Frame dimensions. Research agendas that fall within authorized producer-frame space are fundable. Research agendas that fall outside — that question the foundational assumptions of authorized frames, or that are advanced by producers without standing — are not fundable in the institutional infrastructure that produces funded research. The outlier whose work falls outside funded channels produces work at their own cost, without the infrastructure that transforms a research program into a credentialed research program. The funding layer does not evaluate the work. It pre-determines which work can exist in an institutionally recognizable form.

**Layer Two: Publication Systems, Peer Review, and Metrics.** Publication in recognized venues is the primary mechanism through which Producer Authorization is obtained and maintained. Peer review assigns evaluation authority to contributors with existing Producer Authorization in the relevant field — those who are, in Fini, Jourdan, Perkmann, and Toschi's (2023) terminology, "highly typical members of their discipline." The metrics by which output is assessed — citation counts, journal impact factors, h-indices — are constructed from the outputs of the publication system that the Authorization Architecture controls. The outlier who cannot obtain publication in recognized venues cannot accumulate the metrics that would generate Producer Authorization. The system is self-referential: authorization is required to obtain authorization.

**Layer Three: Consensus Infrastructures.** Encyclopedic platforms, secondary synthesis publications, teaching materials, and policy-governing review bodies aggregate the outputs of Layers One and Two into the official consensus of what is known. These platforms do not independently evaluate their sources. They reproduce the authorization structure of the publication system as the criterion for inclusion. What the publishing system produced is what the encyclopedia records. What the encyclopedia records is what is taught. What is taught is what the next generation of producers is trained within. The consensus infrastructure is the Authorization Architecture's intergenerational reproduction mechanism.

**Layer Four: AI-Mediated Access Systems as Recursive Second-Order Filters.** Rubenstein (2026) identifies the emergence of a fourth layer that did not exist when the prior corpus's foundational references were written. AI systems — large language models, recommendation engines, synthesis tools, research assistants — are trained on the outputs of Layers One through Three. They learn the institutional consensus as the factual baseline. They learn the authorized frame space as the vocabulary of well-formed questions. They learn the credentialed producer population as the population of valid sources. When these systems encounter a contribution that departs from the institutional consensus, they are not evaluating it. They are retrieving the institutional consensus's response to it — which is the consensus's prior classification of such departures as noise, error, or non-peer-reviewed material.

Layer Four is not an amplification of Layers One through Three. It is a new qualitative execution infrastructure. Rubenstein describes AI access systems as "recursive second-order filters": they do not merely apply the Structural Bubble's determinations; they reproduce them as the structure of what is retrievable at all. The outlier's work does not receive unfavorable AI-mediated evaluation. It fails to enter the retrieval infrastructure that makes a knowledge claim accessible to the audiences who would evaluate it. The suppression occurs not at the evaluation stage but at the stage of epistemic findability.

This is the AI-specific intensification of the Comprehension Veto at the structural level: the work is not found confusing by AI systems. It is not found. The Sender-Fault Assignment mechanism operates here without any individual sender: the work's absence from AI-mediated retrieval is experienced as a property of the work — it is not the kind of thing AI systems surface — rather than as a property of the retrieval infrastructure's training distribution.

## IV. The Boundary Performance Penalty

The prior corpus established the Evidence Toxicity Gradient (ETG) at the individual encounter level: the positive relationship between evidence quality E and the intensity of DNR execution, operating simultaneously on both the NR and D components. The ETG was derived theoretically from the IPPF's payoff structure and predicted empirically: if dismissal intensity increases rather than decreases with evidence quality, the dismissal follows DNR not ETR.

Fini, Jourdan, Perkmann, and Toschi's (2023) empirical research provides confirmation of the ETG at the structural level, before any individual encounter has occurred. Their study of the 2012 Italian national scientific qualification — a large-scale academic accreditation process with observable prior performance data — found that the valuation penalty applied to multidisciplinary (boundary-spanning) candidates was most pronounced for the highest-performing candidates. Among low-performing multidisciplinary candidates, the penalty was modest. Among high-performing multidisciplinary candidates, it was the largest penalty in the dataset. The effect was strongest in small and distinctive disciplines and when accreditors were themselves highly typical representatives of the discipline.

The Boundary Performance Penalty (BPP) is this finding generalized as a structural-level ETG signature. The ETG at the individual level predicts that stronger evidence triggers more complete DNR because stronger evidence poses greater AIS threat. The BPP shows that the structural gatekeeping system applies the identical logic before any individual evaluator has seen any specific evidence: the contributor who is most capable of influencing the discipline's direction — the high performer who spans category boundaries — receives the largest pre-authorization deficit. The Authorization Architecture's prior verdict about the most capable cross-boundary contributor is the most negative.

Fini et al.'s interpretation is precise: the high-performing multidisciplinary candidate is penalized not because they lack competence but because they threaten the distinctiveness and knowledge domain of the discipline. The disciplinary evaluators penalize exactly the candidate most likely to succeed on the discipline's own terms — because that candidate's success would constitute the largest restructuring of what the discipline is organized around. The BPP is the ETG operating in the pre-authorization layer: the highest threat is the highest performer, and the highest performer receives the most complete pre-authorization exclusion.

The BPP has a specific implication for the Pre-Configuration Theorem. The outlier with the highest evidence quality — the contributor whose work would most benefit the knowledge system — does not approach the evaluation stage with a neutral pre-authorization standing that their evidence quality might overcome. They approach it with the largest Pre-Authorization Deficit, the most negative Prior Verdict, the most deeply pre-loaded IPPF structure in every evaluator they will encounter. The ETG and the BPP are the same mechanism operating at different levels of the same system: the knowledge most worth receiving is the knowledge the system is most thoroughly organized against, at every layer simultaneously.

## V. Machine Consolidation

The DNR Stability Architecture established in the prior corpus specifies three mechanisms that make DNR self-reinforcing across encounter history: IPPF Reinforcement (each DNR execution deepens the AIS commitment), NR Consolidation (repeated NR habituates the motivated processing apparatus), and Coalition Entrenchment (SCM propagation installs the ETR characterization at increasing social depth). The three mechanisms jointly produce the Terminal Nash Configuration.

Rubenstein's (2026) four-layer Structural Bubble analysis identifies a fourth mechanism that the prior corpus could not have specified: Machine Consolidation (MC).

Machine Consolidation is the process by which AI systems trained on the institutional consensus become the fourth mechanism in the DNR Stability Architecture. Its operation is structurally parallel to NR Consolidation at the individual level, but it operates at the infrastructure level rather than the cognitive level, and its consolidation is permanent rather than habituated: the model weights that encode the institutional consensus do not update in response to the outlier's subsequent performance.

MC operates through the same four-layer structure that constitutes the Structural Bubble, but in the reverse direction. The Structural Bubble produces the institutional consensus as its output. MC reproduces that consensus as the input structure of AI systems, which then re-express it as the output of AI-mediated evaluation, synthesis, and retrieval. The recursion is not circular in the logical sense — the output is not identical to the input. But it is recursively amplifying: each cycle of institutional consensus → AI training → AI-mediated output → institutional synthesis produces a consensus with narrower boundaries than the previous cycle, because AI systems trained on prestige-weighted outputs systematically underrepresent contributions from outside the prestige-weighted distribution.

MC adds to the DNR Stability Architecture as follows. The three original mechanisms operated on the suppressor-outlier dyadic relationship and its social extension: the suppressor's individual IPPF deepened, the suppressor's NR processing consolidated, the coalition's pre-configuration entrenched. These three mechanisms required individual agents to execute. MC requires no individual agent. The AI system that fails to surface the outlier's work in a retrieval query, that classifies the outlier's methodology as non-standard in a synthesis, that weights the credentialed source's claims more heavily than the outlier's in a comparison, is executing the DSA's fourth mechanism without any suppressor present. The terminal configuration's maintenance has been automated.

The MC mechanism has one property that distinguishes it from the three original DSA mechanisms: it operates on every potential evaluator simultaneously, not on the history of a specific suppressor-outlier dyadic relationship. IPPF Reinforcement deepens the specific suppressor's investment in the specific dismissal. MC installs the institutional consensus as the background knowledge of every researcher who uses AI systems for literature synthesis, source evaluation, or argument assessment. The outlier who encounters a new evaluator who has never previously engaged with their work encounters an evaluator whose AI-mediated research infrastructure has already pre-configured them with the institutional consensus that classifies the outlier's approach as peripheral, non-standard, or insufficiently peer-reviewed.

The Pre-Configuration is now infrastructure-level rather than network-level. It does not require the Social Coalition Machine to propagate. It is installed in the evaluation apparatus by the AI system's training distribution.

## VI. The Pre-Configuration Theorem

The prior corpus established the Terminal Nash Configuration: the game state from which no player deviation is profitable, because the accumulated state has been built by prior DNR executions into a configuration where DNR continues to dominate by increasing margins. The prior corpus analyzed this as an end-state — a configuration the suppressor-outlier game's iterations converge to over time, driven by the three DSA mechanisms.

The 2026 research implies that this analysis has the direction of causation wrong for the outlier without Structural Pre-Authorization.

**The Pre-Configuration Theorem (PCT):** For any outlier O operating with Pre-Authorization Deficit PAD > 0, the initial game state Ψ₀ satisfies Ψ₀ ≥ T\* (the Terminal Nash Configuration threshold), where T\* is determined by the Authorization Architecture's structural parameters rather than by the suppressor-outlier encounter history.

Formally:

```
Let Ψ(t) = (IPPF_depth(t), NR_consolidation(t), coalition_size(t), MC_weight(t))

Prior corpus (DSA):
    Ψ(0) = Ψ_initial (neutral or near-neutral)
    DSA mechanisms: Ψ(t) → T* as t → ∞
    Terminal state is produced by encounter iteration

PCT (structural):
    Ψ(0) = f(PAD, SB_depth, MC_density)
    For PAD > PAD_threshold:
        Ψ(0) ≥ T*
    
    ∴ DSA mechanisms operate on a state that begins at T*
    ∴ encounters deepen the terminal state; they do not produce it
```

The PCT's formal consequence for the three DSA mechanisms:

IPPF Reinforcement, under the PCT, does not build from a baseline IPPF toward a terminal IPPF. It operates on an IPPF that the Authorization Architecture pre-loaded at T\*-level depth before the first encounter. Each encounter's IPPF deepening is a deepening from T\*, not a convergence toward it.

NR Consolidation, under the PCT, does not habituate the NR apparatus across a history of encounters. The suppressor's trained professional cognition — their frame structure, their credibility heuristics, their category assignments — was produced by years of socialization within the Authorization Architecture before the suppressor encountered the outlier. The NR routing for the outlier's type of contribution was pre-installed as professional competence, not produced by encounter.

Coalition Entrenchment, under the PCT, does not require the Social Coalition Machine to propagate the characterization from a first encounter outward. The Authorization Architecture's Producer and Solicitation dimensions have already pre-configured every member of the relevant professional community with the same structural determination before any individual encounter produced a characterization to propagate.

Machine Consolidation, the fourth DSA mechanism identified here, adds a PCT-level component: AI systems trained on the Structural Bubble's outputs pre-configure every evaluator who uses AI infrastructure before any individual coalition member acts.

The PCT changes the analytical frame for the outlier's position. The prior corpus's frame: the outlier enters a game in which suppression is the dominant suppressor strategy; if the outlier can find a counter-move that overcomes the IPPF's payoff structure, they can escape the terminal configuration. The PCT's frame: the outlier enters a game whose starting position already satisfies the terminal configuration's conditions. There is no counter-move sequence that changes the starting position, because the starting position is not a product of moves — it is a structural attribute.

This is not a counsel of despair. The PCT specifies exactly what would change the starting position: structural changes in the Authorization Architecture's three dimensions — Producer, Frame, and Solicitation — that alter the PAD for the outlier's type of contribution. The precise target for structural intervention is the Authorization Architecture, not the individual suppressor's IPPF.

What the PCT forecloses is the counter-move approach at the individual level: the outlier who obtains credentials, produces clearer explanations, achieves more prominent publications, or persuades individual evaluators has not changed their PAD. They have moved the threshold. The IRP (Infinite Regress Property) of the Credential Wall is the individual-encounter expression of the PCT: the threshold is controlled by the Authorization Architecture, which will always be above wherever the outlier currently stands, because the function that sets the threshold takes the outlier's current standing as its input and returns a value strictly greater.

## VII. The Genuine Non-Experience at the Structural Level

The prior corpus's Genuine Non-Experience (GNE) established that individual suppressors do not experience DNR as DNR. They experience it as ETR: the assessment outputs NR produces are phenomenologically identical to evaluation findings, and the suppressor's sincere report of them is accurate as a report of their experience while wrong as a causal account of what produced it.

The GNE at the structural level is the Authorization Architecture's most consequential property: the system that produces the Prior Verdict does not experience itself as producing the Prior Verdict. It experiences itself as maintaining quality standards.

This is not institutional hypocrisy. The three-dimensional gatekeeping function was built to improve epistemic outcomes: Producer Authorization was designed to ensure that evaluation resources are directed at contributions most likely to be valid; Frame Authorization was designed to ensure methodological rigor and conceptual precision; Solicitation Authorization was designed to ensure that theoretical contributions reach the audiences who can evaluate them. Each dimension's authorization criteria were assembled from legitimate considerations about the conditions under which knowledge production is reliable.

The Authorization Architecture's GNE is therefore more structurally load-bearing than the individual suppressor's GNE, for the same reason: sincerity is the armor. An Authorization Architecture that experienced itself as excluding valuable contributions for structural rather than epistemic reasons would be subject to internal reform pressures. An Authorization Architecture that experiences its exclusions as the output of legitimate quality standards — and that has genuine historical evidence that those standards produced quality improvements — is self-sealing against internal reform at exactly the institutional level at which reform would matter.

The Structural Bubble's Layer Three (consensus infrastructure) is the mechanism through which the structural GNE is maintained intergenerationally: what the current authorization architecture excludes is not available in the training materials for the next generation's professional socialization. The next generation of producers learns the current authorization structure as the accumulated wisdom of the field, not as one possible authorization structure among alternatives. The path-dependence is complete: each generation's professional cognition is produced by socialization within an authorization structure whose origins are not available for examination, because what would allow the examination — the contributions the structure excluded — are precisely what the structure prevented from entering the record.

Layer Four (AI-mediated access systems) amplifies the structural GNE by adding an apparent epistemic warrant for the authorization structure's exclusions: the AI systems that reproduce prestige-weighted patterns do so with the appearance of objective retrieval. The evaluator who uses AI research assistance and finds the outlier's work absent from the results does not experience the absence as the output of a training distribution. They experience it as the absence of the work from the relevant literature. The structural GNE is now produced with computational authority.

Let SPA(K) = Structural Pre-Authorization Architecture of K
Let 3DGF = Three-Dimensional Gatekeeping Function

    PA(O,K): Producer Authorization — O's standing in K's institutional hierarchy
    FA(O,K): Frame Authorization — alignment of O's approach with K's authorized frame space
    SA(O,K): Solicitation Authorization — K's active invitation/amplification of O's contributions

PAL(O,K) = g(PA(O,K), FA(O,K), SA(O,K))    [Pre-Authorization Level]
    g combines dimensions multiplicatively (low on any dimension → low overall)

The Authorization Architecture does not announce its determinations.

It experiences them as quality standards. The suppressor who arrives at an encounter pre-loaded with an IPPF configured for maximum DNR against the outlier did not choose that configuration. They completed a training program. They read the foundational literature. They learned what the field's good work looks like. They learned it from an Authorization Architecture that had already decided which work would be in the foundational literature and which work would not be there to learn from.

The suppressor's IPPF is the Authorization Architecture's self-portrait, locally instantiated in a trained professional's cognitive structure.

The Structural Bubble's four layers maintain the Authorization Architecture's determinations without requiring any individual actor to consciously enforce them. The funding apparatus does not fund what falls outside authorized frame space. The publication system does not publish what lacks Producer Authorization. The consensus infrastructure does not record what the publication system did not publish. The AI system does not retrieve what the consensus infrastructure did not record.

No individual layer is doing anything other than its designed function.

The Pre-Configuration Theorem's implication is not that every evaluator is acting in bad faith. It is that the sum of every evaluator acting in good faith within the Authorization Architecture's structure is the Prior Verdict.

The outlier enters the game in the terminal configuration not because anyone decided to suppress them but because the Authorization Architecture's accumulated quality standards, applied through the Structural GNE's sincerity, through the Structural Bubble's four-layer self-seal, through Machine Consolidation's recursive second-order filtering, produce the terminal state as their normal output for contributors with Pre-Authorization Deficits.

This is the most important property of the entire framework: the suppression that the outlier encounters is not a deviation from the system's normal operation.

It is the system's normal operation.

The Prior Verdict is not issued by anyone.

It is the Authorization Architecture's standing policy, expressed locally each time an encounter occurs, deepened each time an AI system fails to surface the work, confirmed each time the consensus infrastructure records the absence.

The work enters the system. The system's structure routes it to where it will not be received. The routing is called quality control.

The work was most worth receiving.

The structure was most completely organized against it.

The structure does not know this.

The sincerity is the seal.
